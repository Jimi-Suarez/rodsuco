I found something interesting
Spotted a product on Amazon, Alibaba, or in a competitor's listing
▼
1
Search the product keyword on Amazon.es
Use the main keyword a buyer would search. Not the brand name — the generic product. E.g. "caja organizadora cocina" not "Brabantia".
2
Run Helium 10 Xray on the results page
Open the Chrome extension, click Xray. You're looking for: 3+ sellers doing €1,000+/month, no single brand with more than 50% of revenue, and review counts under 150 across most listings.
3
Download the Xray CSV
Hit export in Xray. The validator will read the price, reviews, and fulfillment automatically — no manual entry needed.
1
Search in English with broad terms
Alibaba responds better to short generic English terms. "kitchen organizer" beats "organizador cocina". Try 2–3 variations.
2
Filter for Trade Assurance + MOQ under 500
Trade Assurance protects your payment. MOQ under 500 keeps your first order manageable. Ignore suppliers with no reviews or under 2 years on the platform.
3
Do a quick margin sense-check
Your target landed cost is ~20% of your Amazon sale price. So if you plan to sell at €25, you need to source + ship it for under €5. If Alibaba prices are too high, the margin won't work before you've even validated demand.
4
Now go validate it on Amazon
Search the product keyword on Amazon.es, run Xray on the results, download the CSV and bring it back here.
1
Find a top seller in your niche with 3–4★ rating
Go to Amazon.es and search your category. Look for products with lots of reviews but a middling rating — 3.5★ to 4.2★. That's a signal that buyers want the product but the current options are failing them.
2
Read the 1 and 2 star reviews — look for patterns
One bad review is noise. The same complaint appearing 10+ times is a gap in the market. Common patterns: poor quality materials, confusing instructions, wrong sizing, missing accessories, bad packaging.
3
Ask yourself: can I fix this?
If the complaint is something you can solve — better materials, clearer instructions, a bundled accessory — you have a differentiation angle. Note it down. You'll need it in Stage 4.
4
Now validate the niche with Xray
Search the product's main keyword on Amazon.es, run Xray on the results page, download the CSV and bring it here.

I want to start from a personal interest
Altea's Magnet method — find niches nobody else is looking at
▼
💡 Why this works: Instead of running Black Box with the same filters as everyone else, Altea starts from something she genuinely cares about — like "surf" — and lets Magnet surface real keyword demand she can build a brand around. Less competition, more creativity, easier differentiation.
1
Think of a niche you know or love
A hobby, sport, home aesthetic, lifestyle — anything. E.g. surf, yoga, birdwatching, van life, sewing. The more specific the better. You want a sub-niche, not a broad category.
2
Open Helium 10 → Magnet
Type your niche word (e.g. surf) → click Get Keywords. Then apply two filters in Magnet:
Min. search volume: 100  ·  Phrase contains: your niche word
This strips noise and shows real demand in your niche.
3
Download the Magnet CSV
Export the filtered results. Upload it below — the validator will flag the sweet-spot keywords between 100–15,000 searches/month with low title density (few competitors using it in their title).
4
Pick your keyword — then validate on Amazon
Select the keyword that best fits a product you can imagine selling. Then search it on Amazon.es, run Xray on the results, and continue through the normal pipeline.
